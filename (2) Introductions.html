How do Fountain Pens work?

What are fountain pens? one of the earliest forms of writing instruments and has been officially given a meaning of "a pen containing an ink reservoir that automatically feeds a metal writing point" by merriam-webster online dictionary.

Fountain pens are later descendants of dip pens, thus similarities between the two types of pens are noticeable particularly the nib. Instead of dipping the pen in an ink bottle, a fountain pen comes with a reservoir inside which stores ink which reduces the hassle of dipping the nib every time the ink runs out. Connecting the reservoir and the nib is the feed, which is made of plastic and has ridges with two parts: one for letting the air inside the reservoir and the other spacings is for allowing the ink from the reservoir flow towards the nib with capillary action.
